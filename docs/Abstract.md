# ABSTRACT

**Title:** Prevalence of Multidrug-Resistant *Escherichia coli* Bearing Class I and Class II Integrons in Poultry Manure from Vendors in Zaria Metropolis

**Student:** Balarabe, Amina Abdullahi | P24VTVH8002

---

Antimicrobial resistance (AMR) poses a critical global public health threat, with poultry environments recognized as major reservoirs of multidrug-resistant (MDR) bacteria and mobile resistance gene vectors; integrons, particularly Class I and Class II, are key mobile genetic elements driving the horizontal dissemination of AMR in food-animal settings, yet the role of commercial poultry manure traded informally in Nigerian urban markets as a reservoir of MDR *Escherichia coli* bearing integrons remains undocumented in North-West Nigeria. This study aimed to: (1) isolate antimicrobial resistant *Escherichia coli* from commercial poultry manure obtained from vendors in Zaria; (2) determine the proportion of isolates that are multidrug resistant; and (3) detect the presence of Class I and Class II integrons in the MDR *E. coli* isolates. A cross-sectional study was conducted across eight commercial poultry manure vendor locations in Zaria Metropolis, Kaduna State, Nigeria, whereby 152 samples were collected and processed for *E. coli* isolation using Eosin Methylene Blue agar, standard biochemical tests, and PCR amplification of the 16S rRNA gene; antibiotic susceptibility was determined by Kirby-Bauer disk diffusion against ten antibiotics in accordance with CLSI (2024) guidelines, the Multiple Antibiotic Resistance (MAR) index was calculated, and Class I and Class II integrons were detected by PCR using established primer pairs targeting the *intI1* (amplicon ~1,100 bp) and *intI2* (amplicon ~233 bp) integrase genes respectively. A total of 88 samples (57.89%) were culture-positive on EMB agar, and 37 (24.34%) were confirmed as *E. coli* by biochemical tests and PCR; all 37 isolates (100%) were multidrug resistant, with MAR indices ranging from 0.3 to 0.9, and ten distinct resistance patterns were identified, with the predominant pattern, observed in 19 isolates (51.35%), comprising simultaneous resistance to eight antibiotics. All isolates were susceptible to meropenem (100%) and resistant to azithromycin (100%), while resistance rates were highest for trimethoprim-sulfamethoxazole (97.29%), chloramphenicol (97.29%), ciprofloxacin (97.29%), nalidixic acid (94.59%), gentamicin (91.89%), and tetracycline (89.18%); Class I integrons were detected in 36 of 37 isolates (97.2%), Class II integrons in 28 of 37 (75.6%), and 27 isolates (73.0%) carried both integron classes simultaneously. Commercial poultry manure from vendors in Zaria Metropolis is a significant environmental reservoir of MDR *Escherichia coli* bearing Class I and Class II integrons, and the near-universal Class I integron prevalence (97.2%) alongside high Class II prevalence (75.6%) indicates a substantial capacity for horizontal antibiotic resistance gene transfer at the urban agricultural interface, underscoring the urgent need for AMR surveillance at poultry manure vendor markets and antibiotic stewardship interventions in the commercial poultry sector.

**Keywords:** *Escherichia coli*, multidrug resistance, integrons, poultry manure, antimicrobial resistance, Zaria, Nigeria, Class I integron, Class II integron, MAR index, One Health, veterinary public health
