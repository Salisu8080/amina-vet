## Section 3.3: Sample Size Determination (~200 words)

The sample size was determined using Thrusfield's (2007) formula:

```
n = Z²p(1-p) / d²
```

Where:
- **n** = sample size
- **P** = 0.90 (estimated MDR *E. coli* prevalence from Ibrahim & Habibu, 2021 — if verified; otherwise substitute)
- **Z** = 1.96 (standard normal deviate at 95% confidence interval)
- **d** = 0.05 (desired absolute precision)

**Calculation:** n = (1.96)² × 0.90 × 0.10 / (0.05)² = 138.3 ≈ 138

Sample size was increased by 10% to account for non-response and sample loss: **total = 152 samples**

## Section 3.5: Sampling Technique and Criteria (~200 words)

**Sampling technique:** A convenience sampling technique was used. Vendors who were readily accessible and willing to participate were selected.

**Inclusion criteria:**
- Vendor's location was within the designated study area (Zaria and Sabon Gari LGAs)
- Only litter from vendors clearly intended for sale or use in fertilization of plants or fish products
- Only fresh litter that had not been sun-dried or chemically treated

**Exclusion criteria:**
- Any litter that was mixed with manure from other animal species
- Litter not clearly of poultry origin

## Section 3.6: Sample Collection, Transportation and Storage (~200 words)

A total of 152 poultry litter samples were aseptically collected from different vendors using a sterile spoon by a pooling method. Approximately 5 g of litter was collected from multiple points within each vendor's supply, pooled to create a representative composite sample (Alam et al., 2020), and placed in a labeled sterile polythene bag. Each bag was assigned a unique sample ID and vendor code. Samples were packed in sterile containers and transported on ice packs to the Bacterial Zoonoses Laboratory, Department of Veterinary Public Health and Preventive Medicine, Ahmadu Bello University, Zaria, for analysis within four hours of collection.

## Section 3.7: Laboratory Procedures

### 3.7.1 *E. coli* Isolation

One gram of each sample was added to 9 mL of buffered peptone water (BPW) in a test tube for pre-enrichment and incubated at 37°C for 18–24 hours. Following pre-enrichment, 1 mL was transferred to 9 mL of Tryptone Soy Broth (TSB) for secondary enrichment and incubated at 37°C for 18–24 hours. A loopful of enriched broth was streaked onto Eosin Methylene Blue (EMB) agar (Oxoid, Thermo Fisher Scientific) with a sterile inoculating loop and incubated at 37°C for 18–24 hours. Colonies that were raised and moist with a green metallic sheen were presumptively identified as *E. coli* and sub-cultured onto Tryptone Soy Agar (TSA) plates and incubated at 37°C for 24 hours to obtain pure cultures.

### 3.7.2 Biochemical Identification

Presumptive *E. coli* colonies were subjected to Gram staining and standard biochemical tests: Triple Sugar Iron (TSI), Simmons Citrate, Urease, Sulfate-Indole-Motility (SIM), and Methyl Red–Voges Proskauer (MR-VP). Results were interpreted per Cheesbrough (2006) and CLSI (2024). Colonies that demonstrated characteristic *E. coli* reactions were inoculated on nutrient agar slants for further molecular confirmation.

### 3.7.3 DNA Extraction

DNA extraction was carried out using the QIAamp DNA Mini Kit (Qiagen, USA) following the manufacturer's instructions. Prior to extraction, all samples and reagents were brought to room temperature. Exactly 200 µL of *E. coli* suspension in nuclease-free water from a 24-hour pure culture was added to equal volume of Buffer AVL and 50 µL of Proteinase K in a 1.5 mL nuclease-free microcentrifuge tube. The mixture was vortexed for 15 seconds and incubated at 55°C for 10 minutes. The tube was briefly centrifuged and 400 µL of absolute ethanol was added, followed by vortexing for 15 seconds. The solution was carefully transferred to a QIAamp Mini spin column in a 2 mL collection tube and centrifuged at 5,008 × g for 1 minute. Sequential washing steps were performed with Buffers AW1 (500 µL; 5,008 × g/1 min) and AW2 (500 µL; 15,338 × g/3 min). The spin column was placed in a clean 1.5 mL microcentrifuge tube; 30 µL of Buffer AE (elution buffer) was added directly to the silica membrane, incubated at room temperature for 5 minutes, and centrifuged at 13,225 × g for 1 minute to elute the DNA. Extracted DNA was stored at −20°C until required. DNA purity and concentration were confirmed using NanoDrop spectrophotometry.

### 3.7.4 PCR Confirmation of *E. coli* (16S rRNA gene)

PCR was performed to confirm *E. coli* identity through amplification of the *E. coli*-specific 16S rRNA gene using primers described by Lane (1991) (Table 3.2). The PCR reaction comprised: 12.5 µL Top Taq mastermix, 1 µL each forward and reverse primer (10 mM), 5.5 µL nuclease-free water, and 5 µL DNA template (total 25 µL). Amplification was performed in a Bio-Rad MyCycler® under the following conditions: initial denaturation at 94°C for 3 minutes; 40 cycles of denaturation at 94°C for 1 minute, annealing at 55°C for 30 seconds, and extension at 72°C for 1 minute 30 seconds; final extension at 72°C for 10 minutes. The expected amplicon size was 1,500 bp.

### 3.7.5 Antibiotic Susceptibility Testing

Antibiotic susceptibility profiles of all PCR-confirmed *E. coli* isolates were determined using the Kirby-Bauer disk diffusion method per Clinical Laboratory Standards Institute (CLSI 2024) guidelines. Four to five colonies from a pure 24-hour culture were emulsified in sterile normal saline and turbidity adjusted to 0.5 McFarland standard. One hundred microlitres of the suspension was dispensed and evenly spread on Mueller-Hinton agar plates. Antibiotic disks (HIMEDIA®, India) were applied using a disk dispenser (Oxoid, UK) and plates were incubated aerobically at 37°C for 24 hours. Zones of inhibition were measured to the nearest millimetre using a millimetre ruler and classified as susceptible (S), intermediate (I), or resistant (R) according to CLSI (2024) breakpoints. A panel of ten antibiotic disks was used (Table 3.3).

### 3.7.6 Multiple Antibiotic Resistance (MAR) Index Calculation

The MAR index was calculated as a = number of antibiotics to which the isolate was resistant; b = total number of antibiotics tested (10). MAR index = a/b. A MAR index >0.2 indicated that the isolate originated from an environment where antibiotics are frequently used (high-risk source).

### 3.7.7 Detection of Class I Integrons

All MDR *E. coli* isolates (MAR index ≥0.2) were subjected to PCR for detection of Class I integrons using primers CSL1 and CSR1 targeting the integrase gene (IntI1) (Fonseca et al., 2005). The PCR reaction was prepared as follows: 12.5 µL Top Taq mastermix, 1 µL each forward (CSL1) and reverse (CSR1) primer (10 mM), 5.5 µL nuclease-free water, and 5 µL DNA template (25 µL total). Thermocycler conditions: initial denaturation at 94°C for 3 minutes; 40 cycles of denaturation at 94°C for 1 minute, annealing at 49°C for 30 seconds, and extension at 72°C for 1 minute 30 seconds; final extension at 72°C for 10 minutes. The expected amplicon size for Class I integron was 1,100 bp.

### 3.7.8 Detection of Class II Integrons

Class II integron detection was performed using the same PCR reaction setup and thermocycler conditions as Class I, with the exception that Class II integron-specific forward and reverse primers were used (Goldstein et al., 2001). The expected amplicon size for Class II integron was 233 bp.

### 3.7.9 Agarose Gel Electrophoresis

PCR products were resolved by gel electrophoresis on 1.0% agarose gel. One gram of agarose powder (Biolabs, USA) was dissolved in 99 mL of 1× Tris Acetate EDTA (TAE) buffer, homogenized in a microwave oven, and allowed to cool. Approximately 5 µL of ethidium bromide was added before the gel was cast in a mold with comb and allowed to solidify for 30 minutes. The solidified gel was transferred to the electrophoresis tank containing 1× TAE. Samples and 100 bp DNA ladder (DSB, China) were dispensed into allocated wells with loading dye. Electrophoresis was run at 100 V for 45 minutes. Bands were visualized under a UV transilluminator (Bio-Rad, USA) and documented. Band sizes were compared against the DNA ladder to confirm amplicon sizes of 1,500 bp (16S rRNA), 1,100 bp (Class I integron), and 233 bp (Class II integron).

## Section 3.8: Data Analysis (~150 words)

Data were analyzed using Python (SciPy library, `scipy.stats`). Results were presented as frequencies and proportions (percentages). Chi-square test was used to determine associations between categorical variables (vendor location and *E. coli* prevalence; vendor location and Class I integron carriage; vendor location and Class II integron carriage). Where any expected cell count was <5, Fisher's Exact Test was applied. Statistical significance was set at P<0.05.
