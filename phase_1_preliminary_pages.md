## Step 9: Abstract ← Write LAST

> [!IMPORTANT]
> Write this ONLY after all chapters are complete. ≤500 words. Single line spacing. Times New Roman 12pt, justified.

**Structure:**

**1. Background (~50 words)**
Global AMR crisis; poultry as AMR hotspot; *E. coli* as sentinel organism; integrons as MDR vehicles; vendor markets as understudied high-risk settings.

**2. Objective (~40 words)**
State the three objectives exactly as in Section 1.5.

**3. Methods (~80 words)**
Cross-sectional study; 152 samples from 8 vendor locations in Zaria metropolis; EMB agar culture + biochemical + PCR confirmation; Kirby-Bauer disk diffusion (10 antibiotics; CLSI 2024); MAR index; PCR detection of Class I (1,100 bp) and Class II (233 bp) integrons.

**4. Findings (~150 words)**
37/152 (24.34%) *E. coli* confirmed; all 37 (100%) MDR (MAR >0.2); 10 distinct resistance patterns; highest resistance: Azithromycin (100%), SXT (97.29%), Ciprofloxacin (97.29%), Chloramphenicol (97.29%); all isolates susceptible to Meropenem; Class I integrons 36/37 (97.2%); Class II integrons 28/37 (75.6%).

**5. Conclusion (~80 words)**
Vendor-sourced poultry litter is a significant reservoir of MDR *E. coli* bearing both integron classes; high integron carriage rates indicate elevated risk of horizontal ARG transfer into the environment and food chain; routine AMR surveillance at poultry manure vendor markets is urgently recommended.

**Keywords (≤12):**
```
Escherichia coli, multidrug resistance, integrons, poultry manure, antimicrobial resistance, 
Zaria, Nigeria, Class I integron, Class II integron, MAR index, One Health, veterinary public health
```
